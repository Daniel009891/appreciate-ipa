IPA..Whats all the fuss about?

The Most Common Types Of IPA

  • New England IPA - Hazy colour and texture, strength between 6% and 7%.
  • Black IPA - Similar taste to american IPA but with a dark texture and crisp finish, strength between 7% and 10%.
  • Double IPA - Bolder hop profile with a more rounded body, strength between 8% and 10%.
  • American IPA - Usually a more fruitier IPA the focus is on hop flavour, aroma and bitterness, strength between 5% and 6%.
  • Session IPA - Lighter more approachable taste, strength between 4% and 5%
  • Triple IPA - Extremely hoppy with bitter flavours and mouthfeels, strength abover 10%.
  • West Coast IPA - Bitter tasting but sharper taste and texture, strength between 5.5% and 7%.
  • English IPA - Maltier taste with a fruitier yeast, strength between 4% and 6%.
  • Sour IPA - Tarte tasting IPA with a mostly cloudy finish, strength between 3% and 7%.
